Section 1
Signal Quick Reference
All 11 signals at a glance. Entry and add signals appear below the bar; exit signals appear above. Priority order when multiple conditions fire simultaneously: S1 → S2 → S3 → T1 → T2 → T3 → A1 → R1 → A2 → A3 → A4.
🏷️
Quality Badges on Labels (new in v3.1.x)
Entry and add labels can carry a quality badge that reflects the structural backdrop at the moment of firing.
[+] on a T signal — fired while the Volatility Gaussian Band structure is flipping bullish (basis turning up + upper band expanding). Treat as a higher-conviction version of the same signal.
[*] on a T signal / [·] on an A signal — fired from a tight RMV zone, i.e. compressed volatility just before expansion. Often the cleanest entries.

A4

Stock has held cleanly above the fast EMA for several weeks and just printed a new local high on above-average volume. Cooldown prevents clustering. Bypasses entry lock.

📉 Exit Signals (above bar)
S3
Early Warning
Close below the fast EMA with enough active warnings to confirm weakness. Only fires while in position. Shake-out filter suppresses false fires; strong-trend suppression reduces noise in clear uptrends.
→ Trim 1/3 position
S2
Clear Weakness
Intermediate-trend EMA broken, confirmed by active warnings. Only fires while in position and fires once per trade cycle. Shake-out filter prevents false reads on wick sweeps.
→ Exit half position
S1
Capitulation
Deep break below the intermediate EMA, OR A/D Score collapsed, OR most warnings firing simultaneously. Full distribution signal — clears the position state and resets the S2/S3 cycle locks.
→ Full exit. Resets cycle.

Section 3
The 5 Entry Triggers
Each trigger is boolean (on/off). The total count (0–5) determines whether T1, T2, or T3 fires. More triggers = stronger signal conviction.
# Trigger Condition What it measures Signal contribution
1 Score Gate A/D Score in accumulation Composite flow is in the accumulation zone T1/T2/T3 Adjustable threshold
2 CMF Crossover CMF freshly positive Money flow just turned positive — fresh accumulation T1/T2/T3 Momentum inflection
3 OBV Strength OBV rising or diverging On-Balance Volume rising with or against price (bullish divergence scored extra) T1/T2/T3 Volume conviction
4 Relative Strength Outperforming benchmark Stock outperforming SPY over the performance lookback T1/T2/T3 Leadership filter
5 Trend Gate Above intermediate EMA Price is above the intermediate trend line T1/T2/T3 Trend alignment
⚡ T1 Volume Qualifier (bonus gate)
T1 requires all 5 triggers PLUS a volume confirmation — either a pocket pivot (up-volume swamping recent down-volume) or elevated relative volume. If 5/5 triggers fire without that volume confirmation, the signal downgrades to T2.
Section 4
The 6 Exit Warnings
Each warning is boolean. The count (0–6) gates S3, S2, and S1 exit signals alongside the crossunder conditions. Color in the dashboard: green → yellow → orange → red as warnings accumulate.
# Warning Condition What it signals
1 Score Softening A/D Score below accumulation Composite flow leaving the accumulation zone — institutional interest fading
2 Money Flow Negative CMF turned negative Chaikin Money Flow flipped — net outflows on the candle range
3 OBV Stalling OBV not rising On-Balance Volume no longer rising — buyers not committing volume to up-moves
4 Below Fast EMA Close under EMA20 Short-term trend broken — stock under the fast moving average
5 Below Intermediate EMA Close under EMA50 Intermediate trend broken — more serious structural breakdown
6 RS Off Underperforming benchmark Stock underperforming SPY — relative strength gone, sector/market rotation away
S3 fires when:
Close under fast EMA with enough warnings active to confirm weakness
S2 fires when:
Intermediate EMA crossunder OR close under it with warnings stacking
S1 fires when:
Deep break of the intermediate EMA OR Score collapsed OR most warnings active
Section 5
Trade Cycle Flow
How all the signals interact across the life of a trade. S1/S2/S3 only fire while in position (S2/S3 once per cycle via Done flags). S1 resets everything. A1/A2/A3/R1/A4 bypass the entry lock and can fire at any point while the stage is active. Shake-out detection suppresses S signals on wick sweeps that close back strong.

➕

A1 / R1 / A2 / A3 / A4 Add Signals — run in parallel, bypass the lock

While locked (in position, no T signals allowed), all five add signals still fire freely. A1 fires from a tight base in Stage 1 or 2 — catches the squeeze breakout. R1 is the ICT Phase 3 blast-off — a high-volume breakout out of a prior base, closing strong in the upper part of the range. A2 pocket pivots and A3 pullback bounces require Stage 2 and a healthy A/D Score. A4 fires when the stock has cleanly ridden the fast EMA for weeks and just hit a new local high — the quiet trend-ride continuation. All five also fire when not in position — late entrants can use any A/R signal as an initial entry. If one fires from flat, inPosition is set to true and the S-exit cycle activates.

Section 7
Stage Analysis (Weinstein)
Based on the 150-bar SMA (≈30 weeks on daily). Stage is determined by two factors: whether price is above or below the SMA, and whether the SMA slope is rising or falling. Use "No entry signals in Stage 4" to automatically block all T and A entry signals in downtrends.
1
Basing / Accumulation
Price below or near SMA. SMA slope rising or flat. Stock consolidating after a downtrend.
T signals allowed. Watch for breakout.
2
Uptrend / Mark-Up
Price above rising SMA. Best stage to hold positions. A/D Score typically high. A2/A3 require Stage 2. A1 also fires here.
All signals active. Prime hunting ground.
3
Topping / Distribution
Price still above SMA but SMA slope has turned flat or declining. Institutional selling begins. Often sees A/D Score divergence.
Caution. Exit signals likely incoming.
4
Downtrend / Mark-Down
Price below declining SMA. Avoid long entries. Enable "No entry signals in Stage 4" to automatically suppress all T1/T2/T3 entry and A1/A2/A3 add signals.
Block entries. Wait for Stage 1 base.

Section 9
Tips & Common Scenarios
Real-world usage patterns and how to configure the indicator for different trading styles.
🚂 "The stock ran 5× and I only got a T3 entry months ago"
This is the lockUntilExit feature working as designed — it keeps the chart clean but blocks re-entries on runners. Solution: watch for A1 breakouts out of tight consolidations, R1 Phase 3 expansion signals on high-volume blast-offs, A2 pocket pivots on big up-volume days, A3 signals on EMA10/20 bounces, and A4 trend-ride signals when the stock cleanly rides above EMA20. All five bypass the lock and give multiple add points during a trend run.
📉 "Too many signals on choppy / low-float stocks"
Try these noise reducers in order: (1) Raise Confirm flag to 2–3 bars. (2) Set Cooldown to 5–10 bars. (3) Enable ADR% Filter with a 4–5% minimum. (4) Enable No entry signals in Stage 4. (5) For S signals specifically: enable the shake-out filter and consolidation-aware thresholds — these two settings alone eliminate the most common false exit patterns.
⬇️ "I keep getting buy signals on stocks that are crashing"
Enable "No entry signals in Stage 4" in Signal Sensitivity. Stage 4 = price below a declining 150-bar SMA — true downtrend. This one checkbox eliminates the entire class of "buy the falling knife" setups. A1/A2/A3 add signals also respect this gate.
🔁 "A2 fires constantly — it's not rare"
On strongly trending stocks, down-volume bars are light so the pocket pivot threshold is easy to beat. Fix: raise A2: Min RVOL from 1.5 to 2.0–2.5. This ensures only genuinely institutional-sized days qualify. Also increase the Pocket Pivot Lookback from 15 to 20 bars for a harder benchmark. A1 (breakout) is the rarest — it also requires a fresh ATR squeeze and a pivot-high breakout.
📅 "I trade weekly charts — does this work?"
Yes. Change Stage SMA Length from 150 to 30 (30 weekly bars = 30 weeks). Also consider raising MFI/CMF/U/D lookbacks slightly (e.g., 20→10 for CMF on weekly) since each bar represents 5 days. The Confirm and Cooldown settings are especially useful on weekly to avoid acting on single-bar spikes.
💡 "What's the ideal setup for a momentum scanner?"
Enable ADR% Filter at 4.5–5%. This restricts signals to stocks moving at least 4.5% per day on average — your momentum universe. Pair with Require Stage 2 on A signals and the Stage 4 block on T signals. Set Confirm to 1 and Cooldown to 3–5 for quick but filtered signals.
🎯 "How do I use T3 vs T2 vs T1 for sizing?"
T1 (full triggers + volume confirmed) → full position size. T2 (most triggers, no volume gate) → full position. T3 (partial triggers) → half or starter position; add on an A3 pullback bounce if the thesis plays out. Think of T3 as a probe and T1/T2 as conviction entries. A [+] badge or [*] RMV tag is reason to lean into the size, not back off.
🔔 "How do I set up alerts?"
In TradingView, right-click the indicator → Add Alert. Eleven pre-built conditions are available: T1, T2, T3, A1 (breakout), R1 (Phase 3 expansion), A2 (pocket pivot), A3 (pullback), A4 (trend ride), S3, S2, S1. Set to "Once per bar close" for confirmed signals, or "Once per bar" for real-time. Alert messages include the ticker symbol and signal name.
🏷️ "What do the [+], [*], and [·] badges mean?"
They're quality badges on the labels — extra context for the same underlying signal. [+] on a T signal means the volatility structure (VGB) was flipping bullish at the moment of firing — a higher-conviction read. [*] on a T signal means it fired from a tight RMV zone (squeeze). [·] is the same idea for A signals. Badges never change whether a signal fires — they just tell you how clean the structural backdrop is.
🎯 "How should I use the ATR Trailing Stop?"
It's a visual reference — a Chandelier-style trail plotted as a dotted line on chart, color-coded by the version's preset. Pick Tight for faster profit-taking, Standard for the classic LeBeau settings (default), or Loose for longer-term holds. It does not generate exit signals — S1/S2/S3 still drive the exit cycle. Use the trail as a discretionary stop reference alongside the S signals.
🌀 "What is the Power of 3 and how does the indicator use it?"
ICT's Power of 3 describes three market phases: Phase 1 Accumulation (institutions build a position quietly — low volatility, tight range). Phase 2 Manipulation (engineered stop sweep below prior lows — the shake-out). Phase 3 Expansion (the real move up begins). The indicator maps to this: RMV tight = Phase 1 (A1 breakout eligible). The shake-out filter detects Phase 2 wick sweeps and suppresses false S exits. R1 is the Phase 3 signal — fires when RVOL ≥ 2× and price breaks out after a detected base.
